​Here at CASA, we believe in creating an exciting opportunity for everyone to explore space. To ensure the safety of customers, individuals will be required to participate in rigorous training and safety drills. Our training and safety protocols are designed to ensure that passengers are fully prepared for the unique challenges of space travel and that all safety measures are in place to ensure a safe and successful journey for all customers. These protocols are subject to continuous review and upgrades to ensure the best possible outcome for any eventuality.

Mental Training

​Mental preparation is an important aspect of space travel and we offer an extensive program to help passengers prepare mentally for the experience. This will include simulated spaceflight exercises and simulations of various aspects of space flight. Passengers will also receive instruction on handling the psychological challenges of being in space such as isolation and confinement. They will also receive instruction on how to respond to emergency situations, such as how to use emergency equipment and how to stay calm and composed under stress.

​Passengers will participate in a rigorous physical training regimen designed to improve their health and prepare for high levels of physical exertion in space travel. This will include exercises such as cycling, running, and weightlifting, to improve cardiovascular endurance and muscle strength. Passengers will also learn about handling the intense g-forces and physiological effects of space travel through exercises such as water training. The physical training program is tailored to each individual passenger based on their current fitness level and will be led by certified CASA trainers.

Pre-Flight Safety

​The pre-flight safety checks are extremely thorough, as all of our spacecraft are subject to inspections before each flight. This includes inspections of all systems and subsystems, as well as testing of all life support systems, such as oxygen generation, temperature and humidity controls, and waste management. The inspections also cover all the structure and propulsion systems of the spacecraft.

Emergency Procedures

​All passengers will receive comprehensive instruction on emergency procedures before the flight and during astronaut training. This instruction will cover various emergency scenarios, such as in-flight cabin depressurization, fire, and medical emergencies. Crew members are also fully trained in emergency procedures and are prepared to respond to any situation that may arise.

In-Flight Safety

​All CASA spacecraft have redundant safety precautions in their systems, including life support and navigation. This means that if one system fails, there are backups in place to ensure the safety of the passengers. The spacecraft is also equipped with emergency escape systems, which allow passengers to quickly and safely evacuate the craft in escape pods in the event of a life-threatening emergency.

Medical Support

​Medical emergencies when traveling with our company are highly unlikely, but our spacecraft is equipped with medical equipment, and we have a team of medical professionals on standby at all times during the journey. The team is trained to handle any medical emergency that may arise during the flight, including providing first aid and administering medication.
